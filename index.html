"We do on stage things that are supposed to happen off.

Which is a kind of integrity, if you look on every exit as being an entrance somewhere else."

–Tom Stoppard, Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead

I am a theatre historian specializing in nineteenth- and twentieth-century American theatre and performance. My research spans several performance industries, genres, and styles, from circus to opera, from concert dance to stand-up comedy, and from classical drama to immersive theatre. My current work focuses on racial impersonation in American popular entertainment. I study racial impersonation to understand conceptions of race, how these change over time, and how they impact racial politics.

At Northwestern University, I am a PhD Candidate in the Interdisciplinary Theatre and Drama program and the recipient of a Mellon Interdisciplinary Cluster Fellowship in Gender and Sexuality.

Before my doctoral program, I was a literary scholar, a theatre production manager, and a ballet dancer in Italy, my country of origin.
