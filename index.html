"We do on stage things that are supposed to happen off.

Which is a kind of integrity, if you look on every exit as being an entrance somewhere else."

–Tom Stoppard, Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead

I am a theatre historian specialized in nineteenth- and twentieth-century American performance. My research spans several performance industries, genres, and styles, from circus to opera, from concert dance to stand-up comedy, and from classical drama to immersive theatre.

Before my doctoral program at Northwestern University, I was a literary scholar, a theatre production manager, and a ballet dancer in Italy, my country of origin. I came to Northwestern after a BA in English and Spanish from Ca’ Foscari University, Venice, an MA in American Literature also from Ca’ Foscari, and a second MA in Theatre Management from Link Campus University of Malta in Rome.

At Northwestern, I am a PhD Candidate in the Interdisciplinary Theatre and Drama program, the recipient of a Mellon Interdisciplinary Cluster Fellowship in Gender and Sexuality, and the graduate assistant in the Office of Fellowships for the 2019-2020 academic year.
